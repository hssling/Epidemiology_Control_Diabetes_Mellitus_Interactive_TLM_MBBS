# Clinical Features and Complications of Diabetes Mellitus

## Classic Symptoms of Diabetes

### The Three P's (Polyuria, Polydipsia, Polyphagia)
- **Polyuria**: Excessive urination (>3L/day)
  - **Mechanism**: Osmotic diuresis from glycosuria
  - **Consequence**: Nocturia, dehydration
  - **Onset**: Acute in T1DM, gradual in T2DM

- **Polydipsia**: Excessive thirst
  - **Mechanism**: Thirst center stimulation due to hyperosmolality
  - **Compensatory response**: Water intake 4-6L/day
  - **Nocturnal component**: Waking at night to drink

- **Polyphagia**: Excessive hunger
  - **Mechanism**: Inadequate glucose utilization by cells
  - **Despite hyperglycemia**: Insulin deficiency/starvation signal
  - **Weight changes**: May mask recent weight loss

### Additional Symptoms
- **Fatigue and weakness**: Reduced energy metabolism
- **Blurred vision**: Fluctuating refractive index from hyperglycemia
- **Poor wound healing**: Impaired inflammatory response
- **Recurrent infections**: Immune dysfunction
- **Weight loss**: Especially in T1DM (muscle wasting)

## Physical Signs and Examination Findings

### General Appearance
- **Dehydration**: Dry mucous membranes, poor skin turgor
- **Stertorous respiration**: Rapid deep breathing (Kussmaul respiration in DKA)
- **Body odor**: Acetone smell in ketoacidosis
- **Postural hypotension**: Autonomic neuropathy

### Anthropometric Measurements
- **Body mass index (BMI)**:
  - T1DM: Usually normal or low (<23 kg/m²)
  - T2DM: Often elevated (>25 kg/m²)
- **Waist circumference**:
  - Males: >90 cm (high risk), >100 cm (very high risk)
  - Females: >80 cm (high risk), >90 cm (very high risk)

### Skin and Appendages
- **Acanthosis nigricans**: Velvety hyperpigmentation (neck, axillae)
- **Xanthomas**: Eruptive in poor glycemic control
- **Necrobiosis lipoidica**: Pre-tibial plaques
- **Skin infections**: Fungal (candidiasis), bacterial (furunculosis)
- **Diabetic dermopathy**: Scattered circular lesions on shins

### Ocular Signs
- **Cataracts**: More common, earlier onset
- **Rubeosis iridis**: New vessels on iris (advanced retinopathy)
- **Xanthelasma**: Yellowish cholesterol deposits

### Oral Cavity
- **Periodontal disease**: Rapid progression, loose teeth
- **Xerostomia**: Dry mouth from autonomic neuropathy
- **Candidiasis**: Angular cheilitis, denture stomatitis
- **Taste disturbances**: Due to neuropathy

### Cardiac and Vascular Signs
- **Hypertension**: Common comorbidity (>140/90 mmHg)
- **Pulse abnormalities**: Bounding pulse (early), diminished (autonomic neuropathy)
- **Carotid bruit**: Atherosclerotic disease
- **Pedal edema**: Cardiac failure or nephropathy

### Neurological Signs
- **Peripheral neuropathy**: Stocking-glove distribution loss
  - Sensory loss: Pain, temperature, vibration
  - Reduced reflexes: Ankle jerks absent
  - Motor signs: Foot drop, muscle wasting

- **Autonomic neuropathy**:
  - Postural hypotension
  - Resting tachycardia
  - Gustatory sweating
  - Impotence (males), anorgasmia (females)

### Foot Examination
- **High-risk foot**: Loss of protective sensation
- **Ulceration**: Plantar aspect, between toes
- **Infection**: Deep tissue involvement
- **Charcot's arthropathy**: Swelling, deformity
- **Amputation stigmata**: Previous digital amputations

## Acute Complications

### Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)
- **Pathophysiology**: Insulin deficiency + counter-regulatory hormones
- **Clinical features**:
  - Rapid onset dehydration
  - Fruity acetone breath
  - Abdominal pain, vomiting
  - Altered sensorium
- **Laboratory**: High ketones, metabolic acidosis (pH <7.3)
- **Management**: IV fluids, insulin, electrolyte correction

### Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS)
- **Pathophysiology**: Marked hyperglycemia without ketosis
- **Clinical features**:
  - Gradual onset, severe dehydration
  - Altered consciousness (stupor/coma)
  - No breath odor, less abdominal pain
- **Laboratory**: Extreme hyperglycemia (>600 mg/dL), no acidosis
- **Management**: Aggressive rehydration, gradual insulin titration

### Hypoglycemia
- **Clinical features**:
  - Tremor, sweating, anxiety (adrenergic)
  - Confusion, headache, visual disturbances (neuroglycopenia)
  - Loss of consciousness in severe cases
- **Causes**: Insulin excess, missed meals, exercise
- **Management**: 15-15 rule (15g glucose, recheck in 15 minutes)

## Chronic Complications

### Microvascular Complications

#### Diabetic Retinopathy
- **Non-proliferative (NPDR)**:
  - Microaneurysms, dot hemorrhages
  - Hard exudates, cotton wool spots
  - Venous beading, intraretinal microvascular abnormalities

- **Proliferative (PDR)**:
  - New vessels on disc/optic nerve head (NVD)
  - New vessels elsewhere (NVE)
  - Vitreous hemorrhage, tractional retinal detachment

- **Maculopathy**: Diabetic macular edema (DME)
  - Focal vs diffuse edema
  - Cystoid changes
  - Vision loss

#### Diabetic Nephropathy
- **Stages**:
  1. Hyperfiltration (increased GFR)
  2. Microalbuminuria (30-300 mg/24h)
  3. Macroalbuminuria (>300 mg/24h)
  4. Progressive CKD, ESRD

- **Clinical features**:
  - Hypertension in early stages
  - Edema, frothy urine
  - Progressive renal failure signs
  - Associated with retinopathy

- **Management**: ACEI/ARB, glycemic control, BP control

#### Diabetic Neuropathy
- **Peripheral neuropathy**:
  - Distal symmetric polyneuropathy
  - Positive symptoms: Burning, tingling, pain
  - Negative symptoms: Numbness, loss of sensation

- **Autonomic neuropathy**:
  - Cardiovascular: Resting tachycardia, orthostatic hypotension
  - Gastrointestinal: Gastroparesis, diarrhea/constipation
  - Genitourinary: Bladder dysfunction, erectile dysfunction
  - Sudomotor: Anhidrosis, gustatory sweating

- **Mononeuropathies/Focal**: Cranial nerves (III, VI, VII)

### Macrovascular Complications

#### Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)
- **Prevalence**: 2-4 times higher risk in diabetics
- **Atypical presentation**: Silent ischemia, painless infarction
- **Diffuse disease**: Multiple vessel involvement
- **Poor prognosis**: Higher mortality post-MI

#### Cerebrovascular Disease
- **Ischemic stroke**: 2-6 times higher risk
- **Small vessel disease**: Lacunar infarcts
- **Carotid artery disease**: Intimal medial thickening
- **Silent brain infarcts**: Common in asymptomatic patients

#### Peripheral Arterial Disease (PAD)
- **Critical limb ischemia**: Rest pain, ulcers, gangrene
- **Claudication**: Calf pain on walking
- **Ankle-brachial index <0.9**: Diagnostic
- **Amputation risk**: 10-20 times higher

### Other Complications

#### Infections
- **Pneumonia**: Increased gram-negative infections
- **Urinary tract infections**: Asymptomatic bacteriuria
- **Skin and soft tissue**: Deep-seated, slow-healing
- **Tuberculosis**: Higher reactivation rates
- **Mucormycosis**: Rhinocerebral involvement

#### Periodontal Disease
- **Prevalence**: 2-3 times higher in diabetics
- **Severity**: Rapid progression, poor healing
- **Bi-directional**: Poor oral health worsens glycemic control
- **Dental manifestations**: Loose teeth, abscesses

#### Hepatobiliary Disease
- **Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD)**: 70-80% prevalence
- **Non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH)**: Inflammation and fibrosis
- **Hepatic abscesses**: Increased susceptibility
- **Drug-induced liver injury**: Higher risk

#### Pancreatic Disease
- **Pancreatitis**: Acute/chronic - bidirectional relationship
- **Pancreatic exocrine insufficiency**: Malabsorption syndrome
- **Cancer**: Increased pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma risk

#### Bone and Joint Problems
- **Osteoporosis**: Increased fracture risk
- **Charcot's arthropathy**: Neuroarthropathy (foot/knee/ankle)
- **Limited joint mobility**: "Prayer sign" positive
- **Dupuytren's contracture**: Palmar thickening

#### Other Systemic Effects
- **Hematological**: Increased infection risk, hypercoagulable state
- **Endocrine**: Thyroid disorders, Addison's disease
- **Psychiatric**: Depression, anxiety, cognitive impairment
- **Sexual dysfunction**: Erectile dysfunction, female sexual disorder

## Special Situations

### Pediatric Diabetes
- **Growth impairment**: Poor height velocity
- **Pubertal issues**: Delayed/accelerated puberty
- **Neurodevelopmental**: Behavior changes, learning disability
- **Monogenic forms**: Earlier complications due to misdiagnosis

### Elderly Diabetes
- **Atypical presentation**: Confusion, falls, urinary incontinence
- **Polymorbidity**: Multiple comorbidities complicate management
- **Hypoglycemia unawareness**: More prone to severe hypoglycemias
- **Sarcopenia**: Lean mass loss, higher insulin requirements

### Pregnancy with Diabetes
- **Pregnancy effects**: Worsening control, increased insulin needs
- **Fetal complications**: Macrosomia, congenital anomalies
- **Maternal complications**: Preeclampsia, polyhydramnios
- **Post-delivery**: High risk of permanent diabetes

## Diagnostic Considerations

### Laboratory Investigations for Complications
- **Retinopathy**: Fundus photography, fluorescein angiography
- **Nephropathy**: Spot albumin/creatinine ratio, 24-hour urinary protein
- **Neuropathy**: Nerve conduction studies, autonomic function tests
- **Cardiovasculardisease**: ECG, stress tests, coronary angiography
- **Neurological assessment**: Mini-mental state examination

### Screening Guidelines
- **Retinopathy**: Annual dilated eye examination
- **Nephropathy**: Annual albuminuria, serum creatinine
- **Neuropathy**: Monofilament testing, vibration perception
- **Cardiovascular**: Lipid profile, ECG every 3 years (high risk individuals)

## Learning Objectives
- Recognize classic symptoms of diabetes mellitus
- Identify physical signs on clinical examination
- Understand acute complications and emergency management
- Describe chronic microvascular complications in detail
- Recognize macrovascular complications and their implications
- Apply appropriate screening for diabetes-related complications
