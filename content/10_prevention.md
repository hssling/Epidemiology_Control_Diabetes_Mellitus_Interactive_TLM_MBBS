# Prevention of Diabetes Mellitus

## Prevention Strategies Overview

### Levels of Prevention
- **Primary Prevention**: Prevent onset of diabetes in at-risk individuals
- **Secondary Prevention**: Prevent progression of prediabetes to diabetes
- **Tertiary Prevention**: Prevent complications and disability

### Population-Based Approach
- **Universal strategy**: Health education and lifestyle modification for all
- **Selective strategy**: Targeted interventions for high-risk groups
- **Indicated strategy**: Intensive interventions for prediabetes

## Primary Prevention

### Lifestyle Modification Programs

#### Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) Model
- **DPP study results**: 58% reduction in diabetes incidence
- **Core components**: Weight loss, diet, physical activity
- **Sustainability**: Long-term lifestyle changes required
- **Cost-effectiveness**: Higher upfront cost with long-term savings

#### Intensive Lifestyle Intervention
- **Weight loss target**: 5-7% of body weight
- **Calorie restriction**: 500-1000 kcal deficit daily
- **Physical activity**: 150 minutes/week moderate intensity
- **Maintenance phase**: Support through 3 years minimum

### Pharmacological Primary Prevention

#### Metformin for Prevention
- **DPP metformin arm**: 31% reduction in diabetes incidence
- **Indications**: BMI >35 kg/m² with additional risk factors
- **Dosage**: 850 mg twice daily
- **Monitoring**: Annual glucose monitoring

#### Other Agents
- **Acarbose**: Marginal effect, gastrointestinal side effects
- **Orlistat**: Weight loss agent, modest benefit
- **Thiazolidinediones**: Proven efficacy but side effect concerns
- **Research agents**: Multiple ongoing trials

## Secondary Prevention

### Prediabetes Management

#### Prediabetes Categories
- **IFG**: Fasting plasma glucose 100-125 mg/dL
- **IGT**: 2-hour PG 140-199 mg/dL
- **Combined IFG+IGT**: Highest risk of progression
- **Progression rate**: 5-10% annual conversion to diabetes

#### Lifestyle Intervention in Prediabetes
- **Goal Setting**: Weight loss 5-10%, activity increase
- **DPP adaptation**: Intensive counseling sessions
- **Telephone coaching**: Cost-effective delivery method
- **Community programs**: Group lifestyle coaching

#### Pharmacological Intervention
- **Metformin**: First-line pharmacological prevention
- **Indications**: BMI ≥35 kg/m² or age <60 years
- **Dosage**: 850 mg twice daily
- **Duration**: Until risk factors reduced

## Diet and Nutritional Prevention

### Dietary Patterns for Prevention

#### Mediterranean Diet
- **Components**: Vegetables, fruits, whole grains, fish, olive oil
- **Evidence**: Reduces diabetes incidence by 30-50%
- **Mechanism**: Anti-inflammatory effects, improved insulin sensitivity
- **Practicality**: Cultural adaptation needed in India

#### DASH Diet (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension)
- **Sodium restriction**: <2.4g/day
- **Calcium and potassium**: Increased intake
- **Whole foods emphasis**: Reduced processed foods
- **Blood pressure control**: Additional benefit for diabetics

#### Low-Carbohydrate Diets
- **Carbohydrate restriction**: 40-80g/day initially
- **Protein and fat emphasis**: Plant-based sources preferred
- **Weight loss efficacy**: Superior short-term weight loss
- **Sustainability**: Long-term adherence challenging

#### Vegetarian/Vegan Diets
- **Plant-based eating**: Reduced animal product consumption
- **Evidence**: Lower BMI, improved insulin sensitivity
- **Cultural relevance**: Matches Indian vegetarian preferences
- **Nutrient monitoring**: B12, iron, calcium supplementation

### Specific Dietary Modifications

#### Carbohydrate Quality
- **Glycemic index/load**: Low GI carbohydrates preferred
- **Whole grains**: Replace refined grains
- **Fiber intake**: 25-30g/day target
- **Complex carbohydrates**: Whole fruits, vegetables, legumes

#### Fat Composition
- **Saturated fat reduction**: <7% of calories
- **Polyunsaturated fats**: Increase omega-3 sources
- **Monounsaturated fats**: Olive oil, nuts, avocados
- **Trans fat avoidance**: No hydrogenated oils

#### Protein Optimization
- **Plant-based proteins**: Pulses, legumes, nuts
- **Lean animal proteins**: Fish, poultry moderation
- **Protein distribution**: Evenly across meals
- **Portion control**: Based on body weight requirements

#### Micronutrients and Supplementation
- **Vitamin D**: Deficiency correction in South Asians
- **Magnesium**: Role in insulin metabolism
- **Chromium**: Potential benefits in diabetes prevention
- **Fiber supplements**: Psyllium, glucomannan

## Physical Activity and Exercise Prevention

### Exercise Prescription

#### Aerobic Activity
- **Duration**: 150 minutes/week moderate intensity
- **Intensity**: 40-60% VO2 max
- **Frequency**: Minimum 3 days/week, ideally daily
- **Types**: Brisk walking, cycling, swimming

#### Resistance Training
- **Frequency**: 2-3 sessions/week
- **Muscle groups**: Major muscle groups involved
- **Progressive overload**: Increase resistance over time
- **Sets and repetitions**: 8-12 reps, 2-3 sets

#### Flexibility and Balance
- **Yoga**: Improved insulin sensitivity, stress reduction
- **Tai Chi**: Balance and relaxation benefits
- **Stretching**: Improve joint mobility
- **Meditation**: Mindfulness practice benefits

### Special Considerations

#### Workplace Activity
- **Desk ergonomics**: Standing desks, frequent breaks
- **Active commuting**: Walking/cycling to work
- **Lunch break walks**: 20-30 minute walks
- **Team activities**: Group sports or fitness challenges

#### Home-Based Exercise
- **Household chores**: Daily physical activity incorporation
- **Family involvement**: Group outdoor activities
- **Gardening**: Moderate intensity activity
- **Dance/gaming**: Enjoyable physical activity

### Activity Barriers and Solutions
- **Time constraints**: Short 10-minute sessions
- **Cultural barriers**: Separate exercise spaces for women
- **Weather adaptation**: Indoor alternatives
- **Motivational strategies**: Goal setting, rewards

## Risk Factor Prevention

### Obesity Prevention

#### Environmental Modifications
- **Food environment**: Increase access to healthy foods
- **Built environment**: Walking/biking infrastructure
- **Marketing regulation**: Limit junk food advertising
- **School programs**: Healthy food in schools

#### Behavioral Strategies
- **Portion control**: Smaller plates, cups
- **Eating out habits**: Restaurant meal planning
- **Mindful eating**: Slow eating, food awareness
- **Screen time reduction**: Replace sedentary activities

### Smoking Prevention and Cessation
- **Harm education**: Awareness of diabetes-smoking interaction
- **Nicotine replacement**: Patches, gum, lozenges
- **Behavioral therapy**: Counselling and motivation
- **Follow-up support**: Quit hotlines, apps

### Alcohol Moderation
- **Recommendations**: ≤14 units/week for males, ≤7 for females
- **Binge drinking avoidance**: Cultural contexts in India
- **Non-alcoholic alternatives**: Social drinking options
- **Complete cessation**: If addiction present

### Sleep Hygiene
- **Sleep duration**: 7-9 hours/night
- **Sleep quality**: Regular schedule, dark room
- **Sleep disorders**: Screen OSA, treat appropriately
- **Chronotherapy**: Maintain regular sleep-wake cycle
